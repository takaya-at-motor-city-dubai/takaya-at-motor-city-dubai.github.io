Payment Plan for The Forest Living by Majid Al Futtaim

Investing in your dream home at Takaya at Motor City by Union Properties is made easy with our flexible and convenient payment plans. We understand that purchasing a property is a significant decision, and our payment plan is designed to provide you with financial flexibility and peace of mind.

Booking Deposit: Initial Payment: 10% of the property value is required as a booking deposit to secure your unit at Takaya.

During Construction

  • 1st Installment: 10% of the property value is payable 3 months after the booking date.
  • 2nd Installment: 10% of the property value is payable 6 months after the booking date.
  • 3rd Installment: 10% of the property value is payable 9 months after the booking date.
  • 4th Installment: 10% of the property value is payable 12 months after the booking date.
  • 5th Installment: 10% of the property value is payable 18 months after the booking date.
  • 6th Installment: 10% of the property value is payable 24 months after the booking date.

    • On Handover: Final Payment: 30% of the property value is due upon completion and handover of your unit, allowing you to move into your new home.

    Flexible Financing Options

    We offer a range of financing options through our preferred banking partners. Whether you are a first-time homebuyer or a seasoned investor, our team can assist you in finding the best mortgage plan that suits your financial needs and goals.

    Benefits of Our Payment Plan

  • Ease of Payment: Our staggered payment schedule ensures that you can manage your finances efficiently without any undue stress.
  • Financial Flexibility: By spreading the payments over the construction period, you have the opportunity to plan and allocate your resources effectively.
  • Transparent Process: We believe in complete transparency and provide you with detailed payment schedules and timelines, ensuring you are always informed.
  • Secure Investment: With only 30% payable upon handover, you can rest assured knowing that the majority of your investment is tied to the completion of your new home.
